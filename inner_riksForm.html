Full Cycle UX for a continuous feature release

The project

With the rise of fiber and satellite TV, RiksTV has seen a part of its customer base shift toward other providers. This has prompted an internal reorganization to embrace agile principles and team-based delivery, as well as to simplify internal processes from a UX perspective.

My role:

Research

Ideation

Architecture

Prototyping

QA


As a sole UX resource, I conducted a series of interviews, usability evaluations and requirement analysis with developers and customer support staff. This research isolated several extremely old and ineffective software tools support staff uses to log reasons why customers cancel the service. I delivered a new concept for cancellation logging tools using best UX practices and created a streamlined list of cancellation reasons that was implemented in all relevant systems and enforced as the single source of truth.

Challenges

The project contained a large volume of work in a very short period (three weeks) during which I conducted more than a dozen interviews, 2 user testing session, and many feedback sessions to understand the reasons why are customers leaving and the limitations of the then-current systems in place.

Additionally, I created a new cancellation form that makes it easier for customers to painlessly leave the service while at the same time emabling RiksTV to display various retention options like special deals, discounts, etc.
